Welcome to Animal Idioms

Learn English idioms related to animals and their Armenian equivalents

Click on the navigation links to explore our project

This site is created by a group of 10-graders from the secondary school of Nor Kyanq village, Ararat region, Republic of Armenia as a project work. It aims to make the learning of some English animal idioms easy and fun as well as to introduce their origins and Armenian equivalents.

What is an idiom. According to dictionary it is defined as a group of words in a fixed order that has a particular meaning not deducible from the meanins of each word on its own, such as in Armenian idioms "շան հացը կտրել" or "կրակն ընկնել" we don't interpret them directly as "to cut down on food for a dog" or "to put oneself into the fire". Respectively their English equivalents may sound as "to talk someone's ear off" (ականջը տանել) and "to be in deep water"

Expressive and figurative means such as idioms and proverbs are the wealth and beauty of each language. They reflect the originality of a language and folk wisdom. They are the integral part of a language learning and teaching. Non-native speakers of English often find it difficult to figure out the meanings of English idioms. Our project work aims at making both teaching and learning of the idioms easy and fun.

As a subject of our study we have chosen "Animal idioms" which have their unique place and history in the idiomatic treasury of the English language.

The behavior of animals has inspired a vaiety of geological idioms that are applied to people and everyday situations. To make their meanings understandable we should take into account the metaphoric concept "animal is a human" or "human is an animal". Animals take on human properties, they have human emotions and can even perform human actions.
